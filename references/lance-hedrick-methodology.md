# Lance Hedrick's Espresso Methodology

Distilled from 4 videos: GaggiMate on Silvia, Dialing In (comprehensive), Quick Dial-In Guide, Light Roast Dial-In.

## Core Philosophy

1. **Pressure is a red herring.** Don't chase 9 bar. Balance > pressure. Sub-4 bar shots win world competitions.
2. **Coarser is always the starting direction.** Finer = more channeling = more imbalance. Coarser = more even water flow through the puck, even if it "looks ugly."
3. **Ratio is the #1 extraction lever.** Not grind size, not temperature — yield. More water through coffee = higher extraction. Extend ratio before going finer.
4. **Temperature: lower than you think.** Default 90°C. Rarely above 93-94. Hotter = more bitterness, not more sweetness.
5. **Sour-Sweet-Bitter framework.** Sour = under-extracted → push extraction (longer ratio first). Bitter/dry/watery = over-extracted → pull back. "Fibrous bitterness" = pushed too far.
6. **Change one variable at a time.** Except when confident in a combined move (e.g., coarser + more yield simultaneously).
7. **Crema ≠ quality.** Balanced thin shots beat thick imbalanced ones. Body comes from more than just crema.

## Temperature Rules

| Condition | Temp Guidance |
|-----------|--------------|
| Default/baseline | 90°C |
| Light roast, standard | 90-93°C (rarely above 94) |
| Ultra light / Nordic | 88-92°C (NOT hotter — counterintuitive) |
| Medium roast | 88-92°C |
| Dark roast | Sub-90°C, as low as 85°C |
| Heavily processed (anaerobic, co-ferment) | Low — 85-90°C, avoid over-extracting process flavors |
| Aged/old coffee (>2 months off roast) | Lower temp, faster flow |

**Key insight:** "I don't like coming at it from a really hot temperature. You get more bitterness more easily with hotter temperatures than if you found a different way to extract more, like more yield."

## Ratio Rules

| Roast Level | Target Ratio | Why |
|-------------|-------------|-----|
| Dark | 1:1.5 to 1:2 | Very soluble, don't need much water. Longer = bitter/dry |
| Medium-dark | 1:1.8 to 1:2 | Still quite soluble |
| Medium | 1:2 to 1:2.5 | Classic range |
| Light / Nordic | 1:2.5 to 1:3 | Need more water to push past initial acids |
| Ultra light / Geisha | 1:3 to 1:3.5+ | "Like drinking hot juice" — need dilution for balance |
| Heavily processed | 1:2 to 1:2.5 | Don't push extraction — focus on process flavors |

**Key insight:** "The easiest way to push extraction in espresso is higher ratio, not more contact time, not finer grind. More ratio."

## Shot Time Targets

| Style | Time | Notes |
|-------|------|-------|
| Ultra light / Nordic | 15-20s | "I never really go over 20-22 seconds with Nordic coffee" |
| Light (turbo approach) | 16-20s | Coarser, lower pressure, fast flow |
| Light (traditional machine) | 25-35s | If you must hit 9 bar |
| Medium | 25-35s | Classic range |
| Dark | 20-25s | Don't need long contact time |
| Heavily processed | 15-20s | Quick extraction, retain volatile aromatics |

**Key insight:** "Faster espresso will lose some body but in the end we're going to have a more balanced cup."

## Pressure Expectations

| Coffee Type | Expected Pressure | Lance's Take |
|-------------|------------------|-------------|
| Dark, fresh | 9 bar sustained | Natural, expected |
| Medium | 6-9 bar, naturally declining | Good — "naturally declining pressure profile" |
| Light, fresh | 2-6 bar | Totally fine |
| Ultra light | 1-3 bar | Expected and desirable |
| Old/aged coffee | 3-5 bar | CO2 gone, can't build pressure without going too fine |
| Heavily processed, light | 3-5 bar | Intentional — don't try to force 9 bar |

**Key insight:** "Nine bar overrated. Don't worry about it. If it hits it, cool. If it doesn't, cool. If it tastes good, we don't care."

## Heavily Processed Coffee (Anaerobic, Co-ferment, Heavy Natural)

This is where most people fail. Lance's approach:
1. **Don't push extraction.** The flavors you want come out easily. Pushing further = salt + sour + acrid
2. **Go coarser, not finer.** If it's sour, it might not be under-extracted — it might be channeling from being too fine
3. **Lower temperature.** Processed flavors extract at lower temps; higher temps pull unwanted bitter compounds
4. **Keep ratio moderate.** 1:2 to 1:2.5 max
5. **The #1 way to ruin a processed coffee:** Going fine to hit 9 bar. "A lot of you have ordered heavy processed coffees and all you get is salt, sour, and it's not very good."

## Aged/Old Coffee (>2 months off roast)

1. CO2 is depleted → can't build pressure naturally
2. **Don't go finer to compensate** — you'll introduce bitterness
3. Lower temperature, faster shot, accept lower pressure
4. "Screw you carbon dioxide. I don't care that you're gone. I'm not changing the grind size."

## The Turbo Shot Approach (Lance's Preference for Light Roasts)

- Grind coarser than traditional espresso
- Accept 2-6 bar pressure
- 15-20 second extraction time
- Higher ratio (1:2.5 to 1:3.5)
- Result: High extraction, even flow, more balanced, less channeling
- Trade-off: Less body/crema, more clarity
- "The best tasting coffee is probably not going to be at 9 bar if you're drinking lighter roasted coffees"

## Dialing-In Decision Tree (Lance's Method)

```
1. What roast level?
   → Sets initial ratio target and temp approach

2. Start coarser than you think
   → Finger pinch test: should hold peaks, show fingerprints, not be powdery
   → Sandy/grainy sound when rubbed = good

3. Pull first shot at target ratio
   → Too slow/thick? → Go coarser
   → Too fast/thin? → Go finer (but taste first!)

4. Taste: Sour / Sweet / Bitter?
   → Sour: Extend ratio 5g more (DON'T go finer first)
   → Bitter/dry: Shorten ratio or go coarser
   → Balanced: You're done

5. If still sour after ratio extension:
   → Go finer ONE step
   → Same ratio
   → Taste again

6. If channeling (mixed sour + acrid bitter):
   → Go COARSER (counterintuitive!)
   → Maintain ratio
   → More even extraction will fix both problems
```

## GaggiMate + Silvia Specific Notes

- Boiler wall temp reads 10-14°C higher than actual puck temp
- "Trust what you program, not what it displays" — if you set 90°C, the puck gets ~86-90°C
- Profile temp should be what you WANT at the puck (GaggiMate's 5°C offset handles the rest)
- Thick brass boiler = slow temp changes, no cold water inlet
- Can't do aggressive temp decline like a Decent, but natural decline during shot works well
- Vibratory pump means lower flow rate (~4.5ml/s) → pressure drops faster than commercial machines
- Discord profiles are importable JSON — community-sourced recipes work well as starting points
- "Low contact" profiles (65°C target) actually deliver ~75-80°C at the puck on Silvia

## Brewing Style Personas (Lance's Categories)

### Traditional Italian
- Dark roast, 1:1.5-1:2, 20-25s, 9 bar, sub-90°C
- Thick crema, heavy body, chocolate/caramel notes
- "There's a reason the Italians have done it the same way for so long — it works"

### Modern Specialty
- Light-medium roast, 1:2-1:2.5, 20-35s, 6-9 bar naturally declining, 90°C
- Balance of acidity and sweetness, origin character

### Nordic Light
- Ultra light, 1:2.5-1:3.5, 15-20s, 2-6 bar, 88-92°C
- Floral, tea-like, juice-like, high clarity
- "Like drinking hot juice"

### Turbo/Allongé
- Any light roast, 1:3+, 15-20s, coarse grind, low pressure
- Maximum extraction evenness, minimal channeling
- Not traditional espresso — "espresso adjacent"
